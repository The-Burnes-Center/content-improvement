ISBN 0-8135-21394 The Papers of William Livingston edited by Carl E. Prince, et. al . William Livingston was the first governor of the State of New Jersey. He served in the Continental Congress in 1774-1776. He was elected governor by the legislature in 1776 and was reelected each year until his death in 1790. His talent for political leadership enabled him to forge a coalition in support of the patriot cause among diverse and often hostile interests in New Jersey. The publication of his personal and state papers, annotated by the editors, reveals important aspects of America's Revolutionary heritage and New Jersey's contributions to it. The Papers are limited to the period of Livingston's career in New Jersey. Volume One: June 1774-June 1777 Volume Two: July 1777-December 1778 Volume Three: January 1779-June 1780 Volume Four: July 1780-April 1783 Volume Five: April 1783-August 1790 The Livingston Papers is a project of the Commission.
